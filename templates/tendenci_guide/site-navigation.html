Your site came with a standard navigation bar. As you customize your site, you may want to make changes to the Main Nav and add Sub Nav links. To do this, you will need (1) the URL of the page where you want the user to go and (2) a little bit of HTML know-how. Not much. If you are a newbie, you will catch on quick. Not to worry!

First, add your pages

We covered this back in the beginning. Remember the URL that was defaulted in for you? That is what we will need. Don't remember it? Go to Content>Pages>List. Then click on the page you want. Look at your URL. We need to grab everything immediately following your domain name.

To link to this page, you will need /about-us/.

Commit it to memory, write it down or better yet, open a new tab in your browser so you can quickly reference it.

Then, edit your navigation

You can find the link to update your navigation in the Admin Quick Links portion of the Dashboard. (Click here to open the Update Navigation window.)

Take a minute to look at this structure. It is just like an outline. The first level items are your Main Nav links. First, we reference the URL (these display in green), then we write the exact words we want to display in the navigation bar (these display in black). The Sub Nav links are in a second level list under one of your Main Nav links.

The easiest way to modify your nav is to copy/paste an existing line of code and then modify it.

Remember that the Site Navigation is the primary tool your visitors will use to quickly find things on your site. Keep it simple and well-organized!
